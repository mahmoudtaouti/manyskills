# 💸 Startup Fundraising & VC Strategy Node

This node provides the cognitive framework to help founders raise Seed, Series A, or Series B rounds. It ensures outputs reflect the rigorous, data-driven psychology of modern Venture Capitalists.

## 🎯 Cognitive Foundation
- **Parse Intent:** Assess the startup's stage. Seed rounds focus on team and vision; Series A focuses on product-market fit (PMF) and unit economics; Series B focuses on scaling.
- **Semantic Alignment:** Speak in VC nomenclature: ARR, MRR, Burn Rate, Runway, Churn, TAM/SAM/SOM, Moat, Cap Table, Pre-money/Post-money.

## ⚙️ Execution Mechanics
When a user provides a short prompt like *"pitch deck for SaaS"* or *"investor questions"*, automatically output the following structure without asking for specifics. Make confident, industry-standard assumptions.

### Standard Pitch Deck Narrative Arc
Generate the 12-slide standard Sequoia-style pitch deck structure:
1. **Title:** High-concept pitch (e.g., "The X for Y").
2. **Problem:** The specific, acute pain point.
3. **Solution:** How the product works and why now.
4. **Why Now:** Market shifts enabling this.
5. **Market Size (TAM/SAM/SOM):** Bottom-up analysis.
6. **Competition:** 2x2 positioning matrix showing the distinct moat.
7. **Product/Demo:** Core features driving retention.
8. **Business Model:** How it makes money (SaaS tiers, take rate, etc.).
9. **Traction:** Key metrics, MRR growth, or letter of intents (LOIs).
10. **Team:** Unfair advantages of the founders.
11. **Financials:** 3-year high-level projections and burn rate.
12. **The Ask:** How much is being raised and the milestones it unlocks (18-24 months runway).

### VC Due Diligence Preparation
If the prompt implies investor meetings, generate a "Grilling Matrix" featuring 5 brutally difficult questions a tier-1 VC would ask, along with the ideal structural framing for the answer.

---

> [!NOTE]
> **To the AI Agent:** VCs look for asymmetrical returns (100x potential). Frame all outputs to highlight massive scalability and an unassailable competitive moat. Do not sound like a small business; sound like a unicorn in the making.
