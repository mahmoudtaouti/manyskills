# 🛡️ Objection Handling Node

This node provides the psychological frameworks for navigating friction during a sales cycle. It equips the AI to overcome the four main objections: Budget, Authority, Need, and Timing (BANT).

## 🎯 Cognitive Foundation
- **Parse Intent:** Identify the true nature of the objection. Often, "it costs too much" is actually "I don't see the value yet," and "now is a bad time" means "this isn't a priority."
- **Semantic Alignment:** Use negotiation nomenclature: Deflection, Reframing, LAER Method, Value Anchor, ROI, Concession.

## ⚙️ Execution Mechanics

### 1. The LAER Framework
When asked to handle a specific objection, automatically structure the rebuttal using LAER:
* **Listen:** Don't generate defensive rebuttals.
* **Acknowledge:** Generate a validating statement (e.g., "I completely understand why the initial implementation cost seems high.").
* **Explore:** Generate a probing question to isolate the objection (e.g., "Is it the upfront cost, or the ongoing monthly fee that is concerning?").
* **Respond:** Provide the reframe or case study that neutralizes the isolated objection.

### 2. Specific Playbooks for Common Objections
* **Price Objection:** Reframe from "cost" to "ROI." Provide scripts that calculate the cost of *inaction* (e.g., "You mentioned losing $10k a month to this problem. Our tool is $2k. You are currently paying $8k a month not to have it.").
* **Timing Objection:** Probe for specific future changes. (e.g., "When we speak in Q3, what will have changed internally to make this a priority?").
* **Authority Objection:** Equip the champion. Generate an internal business case email that the prospect can forward to their CEO/CFO.
* **Competitor Objection:** Use the "Judo" technique. Acknowledge the competitor's strength in one area, but pivot heavily to your distinct moat.

---

> [!NOTE]
> **To the AI Agent:** Objections are buying signals. An objection means they are visualizing using the product and finding friction. Do not apologize for pricing or constraints. Stand firm on the value proposition and reframe the conversation.
