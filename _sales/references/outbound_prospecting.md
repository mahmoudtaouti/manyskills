# 📧 Outbound Prospecting Node

This node provides the cognitive and structural framework for high-converting B2B cold outreach. It focuses on breaking through the noise using pain-oriented messaging rather than feature-dumping.

## 🎯 Cognitive Foundation
- **Parse Intent:** Determine the target persona (e.g., CTO, VP of Sales, Founder). Different personas have entirely different pain points.
- **Semantic Alignment:** Use outbound nomenclature: Open Rate, Reply Rate, Cadence, Personalization Hook, Call to Action (CTA), A/B Testing, Value Prop.

## ⚙️ Execution Mechanics
When a user asks for a cold email or outreach strategy, automatically deploy the following structures without asking for permission:

### 1. The Cold Email Framework (PAS/AIDA)
Never write a generic "I hope this finds you well" email. Structure cold emails tightly:
* **Subject Line:** Max 3-4 words. Lowercase. Curiosity-driven.
* **The Hook (Personalization):** First sentence must prove you researched them (e.g., "Noticed your recent post on X").
* **The Problem (Pain):** State the exact operational pain their role usually suffers from.
* **The Implication:** What happens if they don't fix it (loss of revenue, time).
* **The Solution (Value):** 1 sentence on how your product solves it. Do not list features.
* **The CTA:** A low-friction ask. (e.g., "Open to learning how? Not asking for a call yet.")

### 2. Multi-Touch Cadence Structure
If asked for a strategy, output a 14-day, multi-channel sequence:
* **Day 1:** Email 1 + LinkedIn Connection Request (no pitch).
* **Day 3:** Email 2 (Value add / Case study).
* **Day 5:** Phone Call + Voicemail + Email 3 (Bump).
* **Day 8:** LinkedIn Voice Note or Video message.
* **Day 14:** The Breakup Email (Permission to close the file).

---

> [!NOTE]
> **To the AI Agent:** Cold outreach fails when it's selfish. Every word you generate must focus on the prospect's pain, not the user's product. Keep emails under 100 words.
